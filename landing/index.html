About Sweep

You signed up for College Board emails thinking — what if Harvard actually reaches out? What you got instead was tens of emails per day from schools you've never heard of, in states you've never visited, all insisting you're a "perfect fit." Every single day.

Sweep fixes that. It scans your inbox, identifies college recruitment spam using a combination of rule-based pattern matching and AI, then archives it — quietly, automatically, and reversibly.

How it works

  • Connects to Gmail via OAuth — you grant access, you can revoke anytime in your Google account settings
  • Scans for college recruitment signals: sender domain, subject line, unsubscribe headers, and known spam phrases
  • Ambiguous cases go to an AI classifier for a second opinion
  • Archive-only — emails move out of Inbox into All Mail, never permanently deleted
  • Anything misclassified can be moved back in seconds

What Sweep is not

  • Not a bulk delete tool — nothing is ever permanently removed
  • Not a general spam filter — it targets college recruitment specifically
  • Not reading your emails to sell you ads or build a profile

Privacy & Terms

Last updated: June 2026

What we collect

For the waitlist: your name and email address only. Stored securely, used only to contact you about demo access. Never shared or sold.

Gmail access

Sweep requests the gmail.modify scope, which allows reading emails and modifying labels. We use this solely to: read message metadata (sender, subject, short snippet) for classification, and remove the INBOX label to archive. We never read or store full email bodies.

What we never do

  • Never permanently delete any email
  • Never sell or share your data with third parties
  • Never store email content on our servers
  • Never send emails on your behalf
  • Never access emails outside the classification and archiving flow

First 100 Demo Users — Specific Terms

The early demo is a closed test with up to 100 manually approved users. By participating:

  • OAuth tokens are managed locally by the Chrome extension and your browser. They never touch our servers.
  • Classification requests send only the sender address, subject line, and a short body snippet (≤200 characters) to our backend for AI processing. These are not logged or stored after the API response.
  • No email content beyond the snippet is ever transmitted. The snippet is used only when the rule-based classifier cannot make a decision — roughly 15–20% of emails.
  • Signup data (name + email) is stored in our database solely to manage access and send demo instructions. You can request deletion at any time by emailing us.
  • When the public Chrome extension launches, stored signup data will be deleted unless you opt into the public product.
